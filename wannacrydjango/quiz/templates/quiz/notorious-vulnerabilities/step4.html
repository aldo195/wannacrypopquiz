What does this mean?

The Heartbleed vulnerability exploded onto the scene in 2014 and put so many websites at risk of compromise and theft that it became a major news story and even inspired Google to start the Project Zero initiative for a more secure web.

How do I check?

Check for the following words in your VA report using whatever filter/search capability you have. In most document viewers, CTRL+F will suffice.

  • 42430 (in Qualys reports)
  • 73412 (in Nessus reports)
  • Heartbleed
  • CVE-2014-0160

For each match, you should be able to see the IP address or hostname. Each unique IP or hostname counts as one affected host.
