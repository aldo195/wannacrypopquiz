What does this mean?

Network monitoring means that an automated system or a human analyst is looking for signs of bad activity (known as Indicators of Compromise) in the data that’s moving between your computers. Typically this starts with looking at which websites/domains are being accessed by your computers, and which IP addresses outside your network are receiving connections from your computers.

Why is this important?

Malware typically has an “incubation” period in which it is present but not yet causing much harm. This is especially true of more sophisticated “operator-driven” malware that requires the hacker to chose commands such as stealing passwords or infecting the Domain Controller. Sometimes days go by between the initial infection and when the actual damage is done. Network monitoring gives you a chance to stop a breach when it’s still small in breadth and in impact.
